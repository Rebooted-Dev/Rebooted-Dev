## Why Community Comes First

I've been a pastor for over two decades. That shapes everything about how I think about technology.

Tools built without community context are, at best, generic. At worst, they replicate the same exclusions that already existed, faster. The communities I've spent my life serving — neurodivergent individuals, faith communities, the overlooked and differently-wired — deserve tools built by people who actually know them.

That's not a mission statement. It's a design constraint.

## Neurodiversity Support Systems

### Who This Is For

The ADHD, AuDHD, and ASD communities in Singapore and beyond — particularly those navigating work, relationships, and daily life in systems built entirely around neurotypical defaults.

### What I'm Working Toward

Not generic productivity apps with a new label. AI-powered tools designed from the ground up to work *with* a neurodivergent brain — to scaffold the things that are genuinely hard (executive function, emotional regulation, the "wall of awful" before starting something) without pathologising the things that are genuinely strengths (hyperfocus, pattern recognition, unconventional connection-making).

**Concrete examples of what this looks like:**

- A chatbot that doesn't just remind you of a deadline but helps you navigate the anxiety and executive dysfunction that arrives with it
- Tools that help script difficult conversations, or de-escalate before a meltdown
- Employment matching that speaks the language of ND cognitive profiles, not just job descriptions

### The Community I'm Embedded In

I organise neurodivergent community gatherings in Singapore. This isn't arm's-length research. These are my people. The tools I build are informed by years of direct community presence — knowing what's actually hard, what's actually needed, and what well-meaning tools consistently miss.

## Faith-Tech

### The Gap I'm Addressing

The intersection of AI and Christian ministry is significantly underdeveloped — not for lack of interest, but because the people who understand the theological depth tend not to be the people building the tools, and vice versa.

Most "faith-based AI" products are thin wrappers: basic Bible search with a chatbot interface, devotional content generators that lack theological rigour. The tools that could genuinely deepen formation, support pastoral care at scale, and help communities engage ancient wisdom in a digital age haven't been built yet.

### What I'm Building Toward

- **Sermon preparation tools** that handle the structural and research overhead, freeing pastoral mental energy for the theological and pastoral core
- **Spiritual formation agents** that support ongoing discipleship — not as replacements for community and relationship, but as scaffolds between them
- **Ministry media pipelines** that make high-quality communication accessible to under-resourced mission teams
- **Generative tools for children's ministry** — making ancient narratives visually accessible and engaging for a new generation

### The Ethics

These tools must be aids, not idols. They should deepen reflection, not replace it. The question isn't "what can AI do in ministry" but "what does faithful technology use look like when the goal is human flourishing, not efficiency metrics."

That's a harder question. It's also the right one.

## AI Uncles

In 2025, I became part of a group we call **"AI Uncles"** — a cohort of experienced Singaporean technologists combining decades of systems intuition with practical AI delivery.

The name is deliberate. "Uncle" in Singapore English carries weight — seniority, directness, the willingness to tell you what's actually true rather than what sounds good. We've been in technology long enough to have watched multiple cycles of "this changes everything" that didn't, and to recognise what's different about this one.

Our focus: practical AI delivery for ministry and community use cases. Not enterprise transformation. Not venture-scale products. Tools that actually serve people who are often overlooked by mainstream AI development.

We combine deep experience with genuine humility about what we don't know — which is, honestly, a lot of what makes AI work at the community level.
