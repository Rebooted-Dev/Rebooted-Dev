## 📖 My Journey: From Code to Calling
<details>
<summary><b>🐧 A Penguin's Waddle Through Tech, Faith, and AI</b></summary>

In 1980s Singapore's recession grip, a 15-year-old me defied the script—trading exam drudgery for code on a flickering screen. BASIC lines unlocked puzzles in a world of walls, forging adaptability from childhood rigidity.

The '90s? Bootstrapping startups, R&D labs, and a turnaround miracle: From 300 users to 12M, culminating in an IPO fireworks. Directors scoffed at my "no-degree" nerve; prototypes shut them up. High-agency hacks defined those decades.

Then, 24 years ago, peak traded for purpose. Servers swapped for sanctuaries—I pastored churches, planted a congregation, and championed neurodiverse souls in ADHD/AuDHD/ASD spaces. Library sessions bridged NT divides; faith became the ultimate API, compiling hearts amid chaos.

2025's AI boom? Un-retirement call. With "AI Uncles"—silver-haired Singaporean vets—we fuse 45 years of silicon with gospel grace. Sermon transcribers, kid-friendly Bible comics, emotional chatbots: Tools streamlining ministry, empowering the overlooked.

It's no straight path—a resilient waddle through slippery ice. From teen hacker to pastor-engineer, life's architectures pivot boldly. Agency? Coded one commit at a time. What's compiling next? Join the sprint.

</details>
