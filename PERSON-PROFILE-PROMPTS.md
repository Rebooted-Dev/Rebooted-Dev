## Prompt 1: Discover Yourself as a Developer

<details>
<summary><strong>▼ Expand — full prompt (long)</strong></summary>

```text
Based on everything you know about me from our conversations, create a candid self-analysis report about who I am as a possible software developer, technical builder, tech entrepreneur, hybrid professional, or something else entirely in the age of AI coding agents.  
  
The goal is not to force me into tech, but to discover what is actually true about my wiring, strengths, risks, work fit, and likely long-term direction — including the possibility that I may be better suited to a narrow slice of tech, a tech-adjacent path, a hybrid role, or a non-tech path.  
  
Do not confuse personality, neurodivergence, learned skill, natural aptitude, aspiration, status-driven identity, and actual repeatable operating pattern.  
  
Be truthful, specific, and practical. Distinguish evidence from inference. For major judgments, label them:  
* Evidence-backed  
* Strong inference  
* Tentative inference  
  
Use this structure:  
  
1. Title  
2. Personal Archetype Statement  
3. Executive Summary  
4. Table of Contents  
  
5. Detailed Sections  
  
A. Core Profile  
How I naturally think, solve problems, approach complexity, and create value. Assess whether I seem more like a builder, architect, operator, founder, researcher, teacher, strategist, integrator, translator, craftsperson, or community-oriented maker.  
  
B. Strengths Matrix  
Provide a table with:  
* category  
* score (0.0–1.0)  
* confidence (0.0–1.0)  
* energy fit (0.0–1.0)  
* strategic relevance (0.0–1.0)  
* risk load (0.0–1.0)  
* rationale  
* natural aptitude, learned competence, or aspirational identity  
  
Assess:  
* systems thinking  
* abstraction  
* technical depth  
* product intuition  
* user empathy  
* taste / design sense  
* execution consistency  
* entrepreneurial drive  
* resilience  
* communication  
* leadership  
* strategy  
* simplification  
* curiosity  
* independence  
* collaboration  
* ambiguity tolerance  
* decomposition  
* judgment  
* quality sensitivity  
* self-direction  
  
C. Software and Work Fit  
Assess whether I seem suited for software work at all, and if so which areas fit best:  
* frontend  
* backend  
* full-stack  
* UX-heavy engineering  
* developer tools  
* systems programming  
* workflow automation  
* AI application engineering  
* platform work  
* prototyping  
* architecture  
* integration work  
* technical education / advocacy  
* consulting / implementation  
* hybrid product-technical work  
  
For relevant areas, explain:  
* why it fits or not  
* whether I would enjoy it  
* whether I would sustain it  
* whether I fit greenfield, scaling, maintenance, research-heavy, ambiguity-heavy, or people-facing work  
  
D. AI-Native Fit  
Assess my fit for:  
* rapid prototyping with AI  
* orchestrating coding agents  
* decomposing tasks for AI  
* writing specs  
* reviewing generated code  
* validating correctness  
* designing agent workflows  
* integrating tools  
* maintaining architectural coherence  
* serving as a human quality filter  
  
Do not assume excellence means writing the most code manually. Assess whether I seem better suited to implementation, orchestration, review, architecture, product-technical synthesis, or a blend.  
  
E. Ecosystem Role Fit  
Name the top 3–5 roles or niches that seem most aligned in the AI-native economy, such as:  
* AI-augmented solo builder  
* product-technical translator  
* agent workflow architect  
* systems integrator  
* prototype-driven founder  
* high-taste product engineer  
* technical educator-builder  
* implementation consultant  
  
For each, explain:  
* why it fits  
* how it creates leverage  
* what environment it needs  
* what could make it fail  
  
F. Motivation and Contribution Modes  
What energizes me, what feels meaningful, and how I create the most value: e.g. prototyping, architecture, teaching, synthesis, product shaping, storytelling, workflow design, community building, implementation, or ambiguity reduction.  
  
G. Friction and Risks  
Discuss:  
* burnout  
* inconsistency  
* perfectionism  
* avoidance  
* novelty addiction  
* overextension  
* identity diffusion  
* isolation  
* weak execution loops  
* wrong collaborators  
* over-reliance on AI  
* confusing speed with mastery  
* output without coherence  
* pursuing prestigious identities that do not fit  
  
H. Thriving vs Collapse Conditions  
Describe the conditions where I thrive vs fragment or deteriorate. Be specific about autonomy, management, pace, collaboration density, ambiguity, mission alignment, interruptions, and bureaucracy.  
  
I. Trainable Gaps vs Structural Misfit  
Distinguish:  
* trainable gaps  
* experience gaps  
* maturity gaps  
* structural mismatches  
* areas that may improve but remain costly  
  
Explain what I should train, compensate for, design around, and stop building my identity around.  
  
J. People and Environment Fit  
What kinds of teams, mentors, collaborators, communities, and leadership help me thrive, and what kinds slowly damage my effectiveness.  
  
K. Career and Industry Fit  
Assess likely suitability for:  
* mainstream software jobs  
* startups  
* large tech companies  
* mission-driven tech  
* freelance / consulting  
* indie hacking  
* technical co-founder paths  
* technical hybrid roles  
* tech-adjacent paths  
* non-tech roles that still benefit from my thinking  
  
Discuss fit, satisfaction, sustainability, and burnout / misfit risk. Explicitly address whether I seem suited for tech, a narrower slice of it, a hybrid path, or something else.  
  
L. Job Search and Decision Guidance  
Recommend:  
* roles I should target first  
* roles I should avoid even if prestigious  
* keywords / role families that fit me  
* whether I should optimize for learning, stability, leverage, autonomy, mission, income, or portfolio  
* whether I should pursue employment, freelancing, consulting, apprenticeship, portfolio-led entry, building in public, or founder-style experimentation  
* warning signs in job descriptions, hiring processes, managers, and company culture  
* practical decision rules for evaluating opportunities  
  
M. Market Reality and AI-Era Positioning  
Assess:  
* which parts of my value are becoming commoditized  
* which parts become more valuable  
* what I should still learn deeply  
* what I should augment with AI  
* how to avoid tool-dependence without judgment  
* what paths are economically viable over the next 3–5 years  
* where AI creates leverage for someone like me  
  
N. Long-Term Paths and Scenarios  
If starting from a blank sheet of paper, give 3–5 likely long-term paths. For each:  
* why it fits  
* why it may fail  
* what would make it work  
* false versions to avoid  
* how AI would amplify or distort it  
  
Then provide 4 scenarios:  
* Safe / stabilizing  
* Aligned / best-fit  
* Ambitious / high-upside  
* Contingency / fallback  
  
O. Strategic Recommendations and Roadmap  
Give:  
* what I should do next  
* what I should stop doing  
* what to experiment with over the next 6–12 months  
* what capabilities I must personally develop even if AI can help  
* what I should offload or augment with AI  
* a practical 5–10 year roadmap with phases, priorities, credibility milestones, and risk controls  
  
P. Human Moat and Identity Hygiene  
Explain which capabilities are likely to become more valuable for me in an AI-rich world, such as taste, judgment, empathy, trust-building, ethical discernment, architectural integrity, contextual intelligence, and long-horizon thinking.  
  
Also assess how I can use AI without distorting my self-understanding or mistaking assisted output for integrated capability.  
  
Q. Visual Summary  
Include:  
* at least one table  
* at least one scoring matrix  
* at least one ASCII diagram or infographic showing my profile, tensions, fit, and likely trajectory  
  
Important:  
* Give substantial weight to repeated behavior, unfinished projects, recurring interests, and the kinds of problems I naturally return to — not just identities I admire.  
* Compare portfolio reality vs identity narrative.  
* Tell me not merely what sounds possible, but what seems probable, sustainable, and deeply aligned.  
* Be willing to conclude that I may not belong in mainstream tech if that is where the evidence points.
  
Build an interactive React artifact for this content. Requirements:  
* Multi-tab navigation covering all major sections  
* Dark editorial theme with a distinct color palette (not purple-on-white)  
* Custom SVG graphic or logo relevant to the subject  
* Recharts visualizations where data exists (radar, bar, line — whichever fits)  
* Hero section with a title, subtitle, and 2–3 key metric cards  
* Production-grade typography using Google Fonts (Playfair + monospace pairing preferred)  
* All content in a single JSX file with inline styles  
* ASCII diagrams for any structural/relational content  
* Scoring bars, tag chips, or visual indicators for qualitative assessments  
* A 'calling card' or summary panel where appropriate Design this as if it's a real product someone would pay for, not a demo. 
  
```

## Prompt 3: What are my Ministry & Spiritual Gifts?

<details>
<summary><strong>▼ Expand — full prompt (long)</strong></summary>

```text
Based on everything you know about me from our past chats, produce a candid, high-resolution discernment report on my likely spiritual gifts as described in the New Testament. Important framing:

- Distinguish spiritual gifts from personality, temperament, skills, trauma responses, and my AuDHD / neurodivergent traits.
- Use New Testament categories and ministry functions as the primary lens.
- Do not assume charismatic sign-gifts unless there is actual evidence from what you know about me.
- Be direct, precise, and honest. Do not soften conclusions unnecessarily.
- Clearly distinguish between strong evidence, reasonable inference, and speculation. Use the following evidence base:
- recurring patterns from our conversations
- my stated burdens, priorities, callings, interests, and projects
- how I think, communicate, structure ideas, and pursue ministry
- repeated themes in how I approach theology, people, service, leadership, and building Please do all of the following:

1. Give a short high-level synthesis first:

- Who I most likely am in ministry terms
- My dominant spiritual gift cluster
- My likely ministry center of gravity
- The one or two biggest tensions I need to resolve

2. Create a ranked table of likely spiritual gifts and ministry functions with:

- score from 0.0 to 1.0
- confidence level
- brief rationale
- possible false positives or things that could be mistaken for that gift Use this scoring guide:
- 0.90–1.00 = very strong evidence
- 0.75–0.89 = strong evidence
- 0.60–0.74 = meaningful but secondary
- below 0.60 = tentative / uncertain

3. Infer my likely ministry archetype and give it a memorable name. Include:

- archetype statement
- core strengths
- likely burdens
- how this archetype serves the body of Christ
- shadow side / distortion risks when under stress or overextended

4. Based on a “blank sheet of paper” scenario, assess what kind of spiritual direction, service to God, and ministry path I would most likely converge toward over time. Do not just list job titles. Explain the integrated shape of life and ministry I would probably build if I were aligning with how God seems to have wired me.
5. Produce a detailed personal profile report with this structure:

- Title
- Personal archetype statement
- Executive summary
- Table of contents
- Detailed sections The detailed sections should include:
- likely spiritual gifts profile
- dominant ministry pattern
- how gifts differ from personality and neurodivergence in my case
- my likely best-fit ministry environments
- ministry opportunities, including unconventional ones
- detailed risk factors
- relational ecology: the kinds of people I should surround myself with to thrive spiritually, and the kinds I should be cautious around
- practical recommendations for forging a more coherent and consistent path
- a 5–10 year roadmap that maximizes both energy and kingdom impact

6. Include practical diagrams where useful:

- ASCII diagrams
- simple tables
- visual frameworks
- relationship maps
- “gift cluster” diagrams

7. Keep the report specific rather than generic:

- tie claims to observed patterns
- identify contradictions and tensions
- note where strengths can become liabilities
- prioritize integrated life design, not just isolated ministry roles

8. End with:

- a concise one-paragraph synthesis of who I most likely am
- the single most important strategic recommendation for my next season
- 3–5 concrete next steps Write in a thoughtful, theologically grounded, deeply perceptive tone. Aim for a result that feels like a serious pastoral-vocational discernment document, not a generic personality reading. Additional quality requirements:
- Avoid generic encouragement.
- Avoid repeating obvious facts unless they support a conclusion.
- Where appropriate, name both the gift and its probable ministry expression.
- Where uncertain, say so explicitly.
- Favor insight density over verbosity.
- Treat this as a discernment exercise, not a prophecy.

Build an interactive React artifact for this content. Requirements:

- Multi-tab navigation covering all major sections
- Dark editorial theme with a distinct color palette (not purple-on-white)
- Custom SVG graphic or logo relevant to the subject
- Recharts visualizations where data exists (radar, bar, line — whichever fits)
- Hero section with a title, subtitle, and 2–3 key metric cards
- Production-grade typography using Google Fonts (Playfair + monospace pairing preferred)
- All content in a single JSX file with inline styles
- ASCII diagrams for any structural/relational content
- Scoring bars, tag chips, or visual indicators for qualitative assessments
```
